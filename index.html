Discovering Stoic Philosophy

My introduction to stoicism was unlike most I've heard of. I never studied philosophy in school, spent time with philosophers, nor cared for it in the first place. I heard names of writers and their books; of philosophers and their thoughts, but always viewed them as too difficult to understand, or not worth understanding. I was a fool.

In time, I learned about The Meditations of Marcus Aurelius; the tome of stoic philosophy. A published compilation of the thoughts, feelings, and insights of a Roman Emperor in his own words, written originally, for none other than himself. Â My introduction was sudden, exciting, and has shaped my mind in new, wonderful ways. I can never explain how thankful I am for this introduction.

But what does this really mean? It means finding the silver lining no matter what happens. Understand: hiding from the burdens you bear will not make them go away, seeing them in a way that helps you, instead of hinders you, is the solution. This is how Demosthenes, a frail boy, with a stutter, quiet as a mouse, can become one of the greatest orators in history. It is how a man is kicked out of his own company can change the world and start a movement of "start-ups" and would-be entrepreneurs. This is how a man, bullied as a child for being different can use his differences to become a dominant force in developing new technologies, leading humanity into space, and reminding us that relying on computers will one day bring its own burdens. It is how a young woman with no prospects, no money, little if any connections, can go from stealing out of necessity, to creating a massive clothing company worth hundreds of millions of dollars. #GIRLBOSS.

Amor Fati - Love Your Fate

The time it took to finally understand this concept (or at least believe that I now understand it) has been tremendous. Amor Fati is to love your fate no matter what the circumstances. It is possible because you have a mindset that allows you to. To love your fate might sometimes seem like a Herculean task, but it is not.
